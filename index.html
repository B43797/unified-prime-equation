Unified Prime Equation

A single framework that pinpoints primes near any integer and yields Goldbach pairs via a finite sieve, a central window, and a tiny bounded correction.

Open Calculator Read the Paper

Prime Equation

Given a center X, find a small offset u with X+u prime. The admissible-first approach plus a bounded correction (Δstep ≤ 2) homes in on the prime.

Goldbach Motif

For even E = 2x, seek t such that x−t and x+t are both prime. Symmetry of residues synchronizes admissibility on both sides.

Minimal Window

Window size scales like T ≍ (log N)², with small-prime cutoff P ≍ log N. The first admissible offset typically equals the true one (Δstep = 0).

What the app does (in plain words)
  • Parses your input: a big integer or a power like 10^k.
  • Computes log N and sets P ≍ c₁·log N, T ≍ c₂·(log N)².
  • Builds a small-prime sieve up to P and ranks admissible offsets with a hybrid score.
  • Prime motif: finds the first admissible offset u and checks if X+u is prime; applies a tiny correction if needed.
  • Goldbach motif: finds the first admissible t and checks if x±t are both prime; applies a tiny correction if needed.
  • For gigantic N (e.g., 10^1000), it reports predicted P, T and Δstep (log-mode preview).

Theorem (Unified Prime Equation)

With a finite sieve up to P ≍ log N and a central window T ≍ (log N)², there exists an offset inside ±T that realizes the chosen motif in primes, found after testing at most O(log N) ranked admissibles (bounded correction).

Corollary (Goldbach)

For any even E = 2x, the symmetric motif {−t,+t} produces a prime pair (x−t, x+t) with |t| ≤ T; the first admissible usually suffices (Δstep = 0), else correction ≤ 2.
